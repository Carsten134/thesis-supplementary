\subsection{Linear Deterministic Trend}
Given the low explanatory power and limited data, we must be very careful in drawing any substantive conclusions from these coefficients and thus also projections.

I also tried modeling the data with higher order polynomials (i.e. $\texttt{freq}_t = \beta_0 + \beta_1 t +\beta_2 t^2 + \beta_3 t^3$) to also capture the mode, many of the clusters exhibited. And while the fit did improve, the models suffered from poor generalizability beyond the given time horizon (projected negative frequencies for the year 2026.).
